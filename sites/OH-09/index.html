Light Teachings

Demystifying the physics of light through real-time ray tracing and interactive GPU rendering on MacOS.

The Vision

Computer graphics often feel like a "black box" to students. Light Teachings bridges that gap by providing a hands-on environment to manipulate 3D scenes using Metal, Apple's high-performance graphics framework.

Users can instantly visualize how light rays interact with different materials—reflecting off polished surfaces, refracting through transparent objects, and casting soft shadows in real-time. Paired with a custom 5-part educational series, it transforms complex physics into an intuitive visual experience.

Proprietary Engine

A purpose-built light transport engine designed specifically for the Metal framework. It optimizes ray-primitive intersections to deliver specular reflections and transparency in real-time, maintaining high performance even as scene complexity scales.

⚙️

Algorithm Visualization

Enables users to toggle industry-standard CGI techniques dynamically. This interactive approach provides a visual comparative analysis of how individual light transport paths and mechanical logic contribute to the final high-fidelity render.

💎

Physically Based Rendering

Implementation of custom BRDF models for materials. It utilizes Fresnel equations to accurately simulate refractive indices for glass and high-gloss specular responses for metallic surfaces based on real-world light physics.

Language: Swift 6.0
